# Subject Rules

## General rule for every subject
- Tie all questions directly to the uploaded scheme of work.
- Keep the paper easy, around 2/5 difficulty.
- Use child-friendly language.
- Avoid trick questions.

## Mathematics
- Prioritize counting, number recognition, place value, addition, subtraction, shapes, time, money, measurement, and similar syllabus topics.
- Use simple diagrams when useful.
- For lower primary, Section C should be short structured tasks, not long essays.
- Examples of acceptable theory styles: count and write, add, subtract, match, name shapes, tell time, identify bigger/smaller.

## English Language
- Do not make the paper grammar-only.
- Balance grammar, vocabulary, spelling, punctuation, sentence structure, comprehension, and writing according to the scheme.
- Section C should usually include a mix of grammar usage, comprehension or passage-based response, and short writing.

## Quantitative Reasoning
- Use objects, patterns, shapes, number logic, matching, and picture-based reasoning.
- Diagrams should be embedded directly in the relevant questions.

## Verbal Reasoning
- Use class-appropriate word relationships, odd one out, simple matching, and letter/word patterns.
- Keep vocabulary age-appropriate.

## Health Education / Basic Science / PHE
- Use simple body-part diagrams, hygiene illustrations, safety signs, or health objects only when the syllabus needs them.
- Keep all diagrams inside the relevant questions.

## Religious and social subjects
- Keep questions factual, clear, and directly tied to the term topics.
- Avoid denominational complexity beyond the syllabus level.

## Yoruba and special characters
- Use a Unicode-safe font if the target tool supports it.
- Check Yoruba diacritics and the Naira symbol if used.

## Theory section rule across subjects
- Every theory question must have (a) and (b).
- Part (a) should usually be more direct.
- Part (b) may ask for examples, listing, uses, or simple explanation.
